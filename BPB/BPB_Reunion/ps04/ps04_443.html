Notes for Ignace Michel Louis Antoine D'irumberry De Salaberry
[DBC] Naval officer. His parents returned to France after the Seven Years War and Salaberry stayed with his aunt, M. Thérèse Juchereau Duchesnay (qv). Petit Seminaire 1765-9. Defense of Fort St-Jean (St-Jean sur Richelieu) 1775, but the garrison surrendered and he was hospitalized in Montréal. Joined forces of Gen. Burgoyne in 1777 which were defeated in Saratoga (Schuylerville NY).

Opposed seigneurial reforms in 1788. Friend of George III's son, Prince Edward Augustus, Duke of Kent, and benefited greatly from the patronage, such as commissions for his sons. Appointed to LEgislative Council 1817. Elected to first House of Assembly for Dorchester in 1792, where he voted for William Grant rather than Jean Antoine Panet (qv) as speaker. Defeated in 1796, but reelected in 1804 for Lower Town Québec as member of Parti Canadien. He proposed a bill for financing prisons through taxes on imports which was passed although fiercely opposed by the British merchants. He was defeated in his riding but elected in Huntingdon which he represented till Oct 1809.

At 60 he wanted to participate in theWar of 1812 but withdrew after suffering a stroke. In 1822, he chaired a committee to oppose he union of Upper and Lower Canada.

He died in 1828 at 76 after a long illness. His estate included a house in Québec, land in Godmanchester Twp, land in Halifax Twp, the seigneury of La Guillaudière, and a quarter of the seigneury of Beauport.

They had 10 children, seven of whom reached adulthood. Maurice-Roch and Francois-Louis died while fighting in India, Edouard-Alphonse was killed in Spain.
