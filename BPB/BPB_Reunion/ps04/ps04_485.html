Notes for Pierre Dandonneau Dit Lajeunesse
43ne calviniste. Cite 1647.

[DBC, Douville] dit qu'il est mort en 1702.

[DBC] One of the most earnest and tenacious settlers in the early years of the colony. He did not even seem to have engaged in fur trading. Settled in Trois-Rivières as an indentured servant to Godefroy de Lintot. Received a land grant in 1652 and became one of the chief settlers of the community. Jacques Leneuf and Pierre Boucher attended his mariage to Francoise Jobin, a fille du roi. He acquired land at the mouth of the St-Maurice. In 1664 he resettled in Champlain where he received land from the crown and bought some from Sieur Besnard dit St-André.

[Barbeau] aurait abjuré le calvinisme avant la traversée de 1647.754

His granddaughter married Pierre Gaultier de La Verendrye.
