Notes for Jean-Baptiste Cuillerier Dit Beaubien le vieux
[C. P. Beaubien] "Il faut considerer JB Cuillerier comme le vrai fondateur de la ville" de Detroit. Il s'y fixa vers 1740.

Ils eurent 3 fils et 4 filles.

[St-Pierre] 716 Il épousa M. Anne Barrois en 1742 et devint le père de douze enfants, qui portèrent presqu’exclusivement le nom de Beaubien.

2 [Denissen] John Baptist obtained 30.5.1745 from the government a grant of land (3x40arpents) of what is now known as the east part of Private Claim No. 2 or the Beaubien Farm. He made this farm his home and left it to his children. He was captain of the militia.

280 [Frank Beaubien] Jean Baptiste Cuillerier dit Beaubien became a citizen of considerable importance in Detroit and it is remarked in the church records that when he died August 30, 1793, there was a great concourse of people at the funeral and that two priests, Fathers Fréchette and Dupaux, officiated. He was the father of 14 children. The seventh child, Joseph Beaubien, was born ... and married... Josette Bondy ...He died in Monroe Michigan.... They had 10 children. The third child, Jean baptiste Beaubien, was born in Detroit, September 5, 1787, and the youngest, Marc or Mark Beaubien, was born in Detroit, April 25, 1800. Both came to Chicago, Illinois to live.
