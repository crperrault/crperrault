Notes for Zacharie Cloutier
Charpentier. Vint au Canada avec la colonie Percheronne de M Giffard en 1634. Sa femme était au Canada avant 1639. Construisit la maison seigneuriale de Giffard à Beaupré. ZC, ne savant pas écrire, signait invariablement d'une hache.

[MSGCF (116) 106-113 pour ascendance]

[DBC] Son arriere-fief a Beauport lui amena des difficultés avec son voisin Jean Guyon Du Buisson (père) et avec son seigneur Giffard. Il le vendit pour aller s'etablir a Chateau-Richer où il avait reçu une concession du gouverneur de Lauson.

> It is said that all descendants of Zacharie Cloutier (about half of the
> Québec population) inherited a genetic weakness that make them proned to

Oh please! The famous "it is said" strikes again! One should not write, even trying to be humorous, that half a population is prone to heart diseases...Zacharie Cloutier was linked to one specific genetic problem, leading to Oculopharyngeal muscular dystrophy, which presents typically after the age of 50 with progressive eyelid drooping and an incresing difficulty in swallowing. Because of a founder effect in the Québec population (a number of people descending from a relatively small number of ancestors), this disease has a higher prevalence in Québec than anywhere else in the world. But more recent research tends to demonstrate that the person who introduced the defective gene might rather have been one of his daughters-in-law, or maybe even someone entirely different..

Zacharie Cloutier appearing in virtually every tree of French- Canadians of old stock, he could be pointed for about every genetic problem in the population. Of course, one has to learn to qualify such "easy" conclusions...
