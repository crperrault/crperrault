Notes for Guillaume Couillard De Lespinay
[Jette] Vint en 1613 comme charpentier et matelot pour la compagnie des Marchands. Anobli 1654.

[DBC] Couillard and Guillemette Hebert had 10 children, and appear in the genealogy of most old French-Canadian families (but not ours).

Couillard came to Canada in 1613, before Louis Hébert, who didn't arrive till 1617. He inherited half of Hébert's estate after his death in 1627. Champlain granted him 100 acres bordering on the St- Charles River. His was the only family that agreed to live under British occupation in 1629, including two young Indian girls, Charité and Espérance whom he had adopted. He took part in the defence against the Iroquois, and piloted boats between Québec and Tadoussac. Since he could not write, he used as his mark a most original little design, which appears on several historical documents. Under Lauson's administration, he was ennobled in 1654. His devise was "Dieu aide au premier colon". He was buried in 1626 in the Chapel of l'Hotel-Dieu in recognition of the gifts he had made that institution. Three years later, his wife sold his house and good portion of his land to Mgr Laval for he establishment of the Seminaire de Québec.
