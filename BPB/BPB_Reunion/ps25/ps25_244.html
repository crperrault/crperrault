Notes for Joseph Cook
Joseph Cook was born in Arrow, now part of Alcester, Warwickshire, in 1822 (baptised 7 April 1822), son of James Cook, a labourer, and his wife, Elizabeth.

Sarah Hawkins was born on 24 March 1830 in Alcester, daughter of John Hawkins and his wife Ellen Mitchell.

At the time of their marriage, on 8 February 1848 in Alcester, he was a labourer and she was a needle maker. Their witnesses were Elizabeth Wright and William Workman Lewis. The marriage is indexed under Joseph Court, a misreading by the original GRO indexers of the actual certificate.

They had five children in Oversley: Ellen (1850), Eliza (1851), James (1854), Joseph Jr (1856) and William John (1858).

Sarah died on 18 August 1858 at Coughton, just north of Alcester, of “diseased lungs, 12 months certified”.

On December 15, 1860, Joseph remarried, to Mary Ann Barrott, daughter of William and Sarah Barrott, a maltster of Oversley. Joseph and Mary-Ann appear in the 1861 census, where he is said to be a hig(g)ler, or door-to-door salesman.

Mary Ann Barrott was born in 1814 in Yardley, Worcestershire (now part of Birmingham). She first appears at age 25 in the 1841 census of Yardley, living in a boarding school but probably a staff member, not a pupil. In 1851, she is living with her mother Sarah in Yardley. She probably had a brother, William, born in 1826. Her father had died before 1841.

In 1881, Joseph is shown farming 30 acres and employing three men and a boy.

Joseph died in Oversley on 12 July 1897, at age 77, of advanced old age and paralysis of bladder. His son Joseph, who was present at his death, signed the death certificate.

Mary Ann Cook appears in the 1901 census as a resident and cook in the District of Alcester Poor Law Union Workhouse. She is a widow, 89. She probably died shortly before June 1905.
