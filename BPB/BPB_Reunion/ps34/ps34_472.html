Youngest son of Sir Peter Spokes and since 1895 has been progressive member of the LCC for Walworth and whip for his party for six years. He has served on many public committees. Other public work includes active participation as commissioner in the public library movement of 1889, governor of United Westminster Schools and Grey coat Hospital. Contested Walworth at general election 1900 but failed; hopes to capture West St Pancras on an advanced poticicla program, which has the full support of Trade Unionists and Socialists in the division. By profession is a public accountant and has unearthed frauds by tradesmen pom certain large London hotels.
