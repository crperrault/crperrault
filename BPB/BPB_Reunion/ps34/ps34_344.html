2097 All of the above property belonging to George 1540-1616, was tied up in his eldest son William's name in a deal made when William married Dorothy Sandforth of Howgill Castle in 1595, in exchange for a dowry of Â£300. The arrangement was that George would have total control of all his possessions until the time of his death, then everything would be guaranteed to pass on to his eldest son William. This was obviously an arrangement that George was quite happy with, and he went so far as to pass on most of his possessions in Ullthwaite at the time of William and Dorothy's marriage. I would guess, and it is almost certain, that William and Dorothy set up home together at Ullthwaite and lived there until the time of George's death, when they would have moved into Kentmere Hall.
