Notes for Michel Gamelin De La Fontaine
[DBC] He practiced surgery at Trois-Rivières, where he was sued by Louis Pinard (qv) for encroaching on his practice. Gamelin preferred to devote himself to the fur trade. He made use of his pharmaceutical knowledge to manufacture beer and exchanged it for furs. His lodges were frequently the setting for most disgraceful scenes in which Indians and squaws indulged too freely in beer while Gamelin wheedled their furs out of them, all the more easily because he spoke their language fluently. Although he was found guilty of being a "trafficker in liquor" he nevertheless carried on this trade till the end of his days. From 1670-74, he occasionally provided surgical services for the Hotel-Dieu de Montréal. His daughter Francoise married Claude Pinard, Louis's son.
