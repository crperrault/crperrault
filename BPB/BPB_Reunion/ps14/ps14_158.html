Notes for Marie Pontonnier
[DBC] Marie Pontennier's first husband was Pierre Gadois, whom she had married in preference to another suitor. The rejected suitor was René Besnard dit Bourjoly, who openly declared that the mariage would be sterile. When the bride failed to conceive in the first year of mariage, Besnard was accused of having made the groom impotent by an incantation over a thrice-knotted string. Upon complaint of the injured couple, a tribunal was convened in Nov 1658 to investigate the charges against Besnard, who denied any association with witchcraft even though, he claimed, Pontennier had offered him the fullest intimacies if he would acknowledge the deed and break the spell. WHen faced with testimony that he had boasted suggestively in public of "knowing how to tie the knot", Besnard replied that he had been speaking about the lacing of his hose. Maisonneuve, the acting magistrate, sent the corporal in prison and later into exile. Mgr Laval repeated the mariage blessing after an ecclesiastical investigation and on 30-8-1660, the required three years after the mariage having elapsed, he declared the mariage null "becuase of permanent impotence caused by witchcraft". Marie Pontonnier remarried two months later. Gadois did not remarry till 1685, but his second wife, Jeanne Besnard bore 14 children, of whom the last two were twins.
