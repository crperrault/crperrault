Date: Fri, 31 Aug 2001 10:57:58 -0400

Hello fellow Montreal listers,

This story about the fire in East End Montreal in 1907 was the worst known
disaster - to date - involving children.

The fire apparently broke out just before 2pm & by 3pm the bravery of some -
had resulted in most of the children being saved.. but tragically - 16
children & their beloved principal Miss Sarah Maxwell - perished - leaving
Montreal just reeling from shock.

It was a 4 story building & the older children were on the bottom floors &
the younger children were on the upper floors. The newspaper gives
conflicting renditions.. but it seems that there was smoke & that wasn't
unusual as they had a faulty furnace. By the time anyone realized the
danger, it was too late to get the children out.. as the stairwells were
full of thick smoke. Miss Maxwell & the teachers herded the children back
to the safety (??) of the upper room. Meanwhile there was an ice house
almost directly across the st..& the workers there saw the flames & ran to
find ladders - to reach the upper window/s. Not enough ladders were found..
but of the ones that were, the men were able to save many children, thanks
to the fact that Miss Maxwell & the teachers..were lifting the children (6-8
yrs olds) out the windows to the men. Though relatively young, this was an
enormous feat on the part of these women as the children must have been
heavy . One story says the fire dept only saved 2 children.. (didn't get
there in time) & almost saved Miss Maxwell..but an explosion prevented that.
Another story says Miss Maxwell just collapsed from fatique. At least 50
children were saved. -- The confusion that followed was "unreal" - as
desperate parents arrived on the scene searching for their loved ones. Many
had been taken to homes nearby.. & the bodies had been taken to the morgue.
The newspaper had full coverage of the anguish (& in some cases relief) of
the parents who feared the worst..& later found their children alive. The
inconsolation of the parents who lost children is difficult to read. -

Then followed a campaign (seems to have been the brainchild of the Montreal
Star - but I've only looked at that newpaper.) They asked children to send
in their donations for a fitting "memorial" to Miss Maxwell. I've found 13
"instalments" so far.. & this certainly will interest all of you who had
family in Montreal at the time... but I can't possibly type up all these
names. The idea was (& here's where we come in) to help future Montrealers
know the bravery of this one lady by making a "memorial" to her memory that
no one could forget. -

There's reams of info..on this story & these subscriptions from children
read like this (all are published) : (few chosen at random)

1. Master Kenneth Carter 4094 Tupper St sends 25 for the Maxwell
Memorial fund

2. Please accept $1. for the Maxwell Fund on behalf of my little boy
George Haslam - Lachine

3. After reading last night's paper , mamma said I should give
something from my savings bank for the memorial for Miss Maxwell. Here
is 25. Gilbert MacLeay - Richmond Quebec.

4. Fred Beaumont sends 15 to the Maxwell Memorial Fund

5. Enclosed find 50 , our little mite for Miss Maxwell 's fund, hoping it
will help to do somethingthat can always be pointed out as a mark of respect
to one of the world's greatest heroines. Nelly & Arthur Wilden

6. We enclose $1. towards the memorial for Miss Maxwell & hope her dear
Mother will not be forgotton - Jane & Annie Pounden (nb: Miss Maxwell lived
with her widowed mother - & it was decided to give her Miss Maxwell's full
salary for the rest of the yr..& then someone else offered to put up a $500.
annuity for Mrs Maxwell.)

7. I would like Miss Maxwell's photo and I am one of her little pupils. I
send 25 an d I am sorry to lose her. She was a kind teacher to us all.
Fanny Pickles - aged 10. (nb; the Mtl Star sent out free photos of Miss
Maxwell to the first 1000 who sent money )

The sums of money sent in - given how little money children had in 1907 is
very impressive.

I think this is too long - so will write out the list of dead - in another
e-mail.

Pennie
