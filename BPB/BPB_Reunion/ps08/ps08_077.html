Notes for Capt. Benezer Murdock Hingston
309 Served as a recruiter and guide for the British Army during the revolutionary war. He lived in Freehold, Monmouth County, NJ on a large tract of land of 100 acres deeded to him by his father-in-law. He also possessed another three pieces of land containing 85 acres. Because of his activities with the British, his property was confiscated and later sold at auction by the Continental Government. The sale took place at Freehold Court House in 1779. Benezer fled the country in 1780, with his wife and children. They returned to Ireland. There is no evidence that Ben was in military service during this time in Freehold. Perhaps he attained the rank of Captain in the Irish Volunteers after he returned to Ireland, in tribute to his service to the British Forces.

1057 Hingston, Benezer Murdock, of Freehold, New Jersey, son of Episcopalian Clergyman, the Reverend James Hingston. Served as guide to the Royal Army and assisted in recruiting. Permitted in 1780 to go to Ireland, where he had apparently come from.

308 [p 44] second son of Rev james Hingston:

Benezar, who served in America as captain in his majesty’s service during the War of Independence. He married an American lady, and had, with other issue, James, major in the army, at present lieutenant governor of Cape Coast Castle, in Africa.

1060 [p 405] Hingston, Benezer Murdock of Freehold, Monmouth Co Memorial London 1784. He is the second son of Rev James Hingston of Ireland, deceased. Before 1776 he had a tract at Lower Freehold where he maintained his wife and several children. He quit home to join the troops at Trenton and was hotly pursued by rebel horsemen. His family were sent to join him at Perth Amboy with only the clothes they were wearing. During the war he was employed as a guide. He went from NY to Ireland where he arrived with his wife and four children in October 1780 and is now in great distress. Claim for 85 acres on Millstone River (13/96/501-505)
