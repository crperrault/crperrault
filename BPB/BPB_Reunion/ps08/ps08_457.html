Notes for Jean Juchereau De La Ferté
[LBP] Conseiller et Lieutenant Général civil et criminel à Montréal en août 1663 (premier conseil souverain créé par Louis XIV). Destitué comme conseiller par le gouverneur de Mezy en 1664.

[DBC] Merchant, member of Conseil Souverain 1663. Arrived with his parents in 1634. Granted land on Ile d'Orleans 1661. Conseil Souverain 1663. In 1664, with a majority of members of the council, he opposed the appointment of a syndic for the settlers and was removed , with three others, by de Mezy. Tracy, in 1666, annulled this action as ultra vires and Juchereau was found witnessing, as "former" councillor, the registration of the letters patent of Remy de Courcelle, Talon, and Le Barroys. Tracy did not seem it expedient to reinstate him, and he was replaced in the council by de Gorribon.

None of his sons was to leave any descendants, and only one daughter, Marie-Louise, got married, to Charles Aubert de la Chesnaye.
