Some corrections (but I know I pubished the same data as you...)

"Robert L. Quinnett" <redleg6@ionet.net> wrote:

>THIRD GENERATION
>
>4. Jehan JARRET sieur de Beauregard, dit Jacquemin, was born about 1600
>in France. He died before 17 Sep 1667 in France. He was married to
>Perrette SERMETTE/SELMET between 1632 and 1642 in Le Rual, Vienne,
>Dauphiné, France.

Jehan was never called Beauregard. His son André was
sieur de Beauregard.

Identification of Jehan himself is a problem.

First impression is he was the same as the spouse of Claudia
Pecaudy. Indeed, there is a period with no birth between 1632 and
1642.

But there is something like a tax roll. This is a list of what
commoners paid to the lord. There is a Jean continuously around
the 1630s. And then, no Jean for more years. And finally, Gonin,
uncle of the Jean of the 1630s died (about 1638) and Jean is back.
This **may** indicate this Jean is another one and son of Gonin.
But I found no document saying that. If there are 2 Jean,
they are never present together. So, this remain a small possibility.

So, I thought both Jean where the same. Now, I think odds are 10%
there are 2 Jean and the father of André would be the son of
Gonin. And 90% that there is one Jean, and the father of André would
be the grand-son of Michel. Gonin and Michel are brothers and sons
of Benoit. All 3 of them are said JARRET dit JACQUEMIN (and Jean,
the brother of Gonin and Michel, and another Jean the son of Michel
is also said JARRET dit JACQUEMIN.

In short:

Proven by documents:

- Andre JARRET de Beauregard m Marguerite ANTHIAUME, son of Jean 1 m
Perrette SELMET;
- Francois JARRET de Vercheres m Marie PERROT, son of Jean 2 m Claude
PECAUDY;
- There are 3 sons of Benoit JARRET dit JACQUEMIN: Michel (father of
Jean 2), Jean 3 and Gonin. Jean 3 raised Jean 2.

Odds are 90%: Jean 1 is the same as Jean 2.

Odds are 10%: Jean 1 is the son of Gonin and Jean 2 is died about
1632-1635.
