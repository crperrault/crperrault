Notes for Rev. Joseph Hull
171 Matric, St Mary Hall, Oxford 1612; BA, Oxon 1614. Teacher and curate at Colyton, co. Devon, then rector of Northleigh, dio, Exeter, 1621-32 (resigned). He arrived in Boston 5-4-1635 with his wife, 7 ch. and 3 servants, and went to Wessaguacus, soon Weymouth. An Episcopalian, and also active in civil life, dissension soon came, even in his own children. In 1636 he was living in Hingham (he part now Hull), thogh still serving Weymouth where he preached last in May 1639. Barnstable the same year, which he left in Dec. Yarmouth 1641, there excommunicated for his act in leaving Barnstable, a breach partly healed in 3-1643 when he and his wife were recorded back in Barnstable. Gov Winthrop, calling him 'an excommunicated person and very contentious' shows him at York bef. 4-1643 till after 1647. Apparently leaving all but his smallest children behind, he returned to England and was at Launceston, co. Cornwall in 1652, called Mr. Joseph Hull, minister, and 10 yrs later was ejected ffrom the rectory of Buryan, co. Cornwall. He returned to his children in America at Oyster River; later the Shoals, these islands owning him 20 pounds when he died 19-11-1665.

544 Refers to him as Benjamin. Weymouth 1635, was soon after, perhaps, at Bass river, now Beverly, in 1643 at York, certianly at Dover 1659-61, of whom less is known than of almost any minister because he seemed in the Episcopal int.[?] It may be, that he went home, and was the puritan minister ejected 1692 from Buriau, in Cornwall. Calamy II.150. He certainly had a son at York and his daughter Elizabeth married John Heard of Dover. His baptismal name was long unknown. Mather III.3 and VII.65. Felt, Eccles. Hist. I.498.

Savage separately lists a Joseph "Yarmouth 1642, forbidden to be a minister (Felt, op.cit). He had been excommunicated at Barnstable merely for going to Yarmouth people as their minister without leave of his fellow-mem. at B. but was readmitted 10-8 or next year. .. It is necessary to follow the poor man to Maine, where, no long time before his death 19-11-1665, he had been preacher at Isle of Shoals, as his widow Agnes showed in her inventory "the Isle owed him for his ministry 20 pounds." and though I regret to say the sum was only L52.5.5. yet L10 is put down for books"
