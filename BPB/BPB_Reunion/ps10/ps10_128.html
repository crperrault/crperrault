Notes for Pierre Legardeur de Repentigny
Sieur de Repentigny. Catherine de Cordé (Veuve) arriva a Québec le 12 juin 1636 avec Pierre Legardeur, sa femme, Charles Legardeur, Marguerite Legardeur et son mari Le Neuf de la Poterie.

[Jette] Directeur de la Communauté des Habitants 1645; général de la flotte et directeur des embarquements de la Nouvelle France 1645-7; concession de la seigneurie de Repentigny (Assomption) 1647, de Bécancourt (ou Rivière Puante, ou Villiers) 1647, agrandie des îles et ilets de la Petite Rivière 1661, pour son fils Jean Baptiste et passée à son fils Charles. (après 1668)

[DBC] The name Repentigny is from the neighborhood of Calvados.

He and Noel Juchereau Deschatelets won over the importnat persons of the settlement to the idea of an association of settlers which would take over the monopoly of the fur trade. With the Jesuits' backing, he went to Paris with Jean Paul Godefroy to negotiate with the Cie des Cent-Associes the transfer of the fur-traidng monopoly. The new company took the name of Communaute des Habitants. The success of the negotiations was largely due to the Jesuits, particularly to Fr. Lalemant who had contacts at the court.

Legardeur obtained funds from Hilaire Leclerc, a treasurer of France at Poitiers. He was able to load 5 ships that reached Québec in Aug 1645.

It was not long before the C des H, under Legardeur's leadership, began to take on the appearance of a family business. All the directors were related by marriage. The people, supported by Maisonneuve and Robert Giffard, protested. The Conseil du Roi reacted by creating an administrative and supervisory council in 1647. Legardeur was made to open his books and it was apparent that his methods of book-keeping were inadequate and that he was not niggardly about expenses. He was admonished but retained his position.

He died of an illness he contacted on ship returning from La Rochelle, "his body half covered with balckish purple spots as large as two-denier pieces".
