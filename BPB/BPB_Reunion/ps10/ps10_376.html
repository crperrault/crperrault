Notes for Michel Lefebvre Dit Laciseraye
[Jette] Charpentier, Arpenteur (architecte)

[DBC] master mason, land surveyor, and builder. He is known to have worked on the second parish church in Trois-Rivières, 1682-3, and the first parish church in Lachine 1702-3 (demolished in 1869).

It would be an error, however, to imagine that Lefebvre was its architect in any modern sense. Lefebvre represented the traditional "form transmitting" folk builder, as contrasted with the modern notion of a "form-gicing" architect. He incorporated in his architecture proportions and structure inherited through apprenticeship from a collective folk tradition (presumably that of Normandy), rather than any expression of personal taste or aesthetic ideas.
