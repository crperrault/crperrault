1661 [Times, 4 Apr 1894] Attempts at Murder -- At Newcastle-on-Tyne yesterday. Annie Scott, 20, was charged with attempted murder. Mr R C Benington said he was an MD, practising at Osborne-road, Newcastle. [Scott, a] prisoner was in his service as a housemaid. On Monday, in consequence of a communication made to him by his wife, he called the girl before him, and said, “Scott, what’s all this about you saying you would like to be your mistress’s murderess”? The girl answered saucily, “Well, so I would like to.” In the afternoon witness, his wife, and daughter were in the dining-room when the prisoner brought up some tea on a tray. His daughter poured the tea into the three cups, an she called his attention to the strange appearance of the liquid. He took the portion of the tea to his consulting room,and, after bottling and sealing the liquid, he took it to Messrs. Proctor for analysis. In consequence to Messrs’s Proctor’s report, he went into his consulting room and examined a bottle which contained a solution of perchloride of iron and found it to have been tampered with. He asked the girl if she had put any of the contents of the bootle in to the tea, but she denied it. That same day, however, the girl said to Mrs Benington, “I should like to be your murderer, but I would not like to risk my neck for you”. The prisoner cleaned the consulting room, and none of the other servants had access to hit. The prisoner was remanded, bail being refused.
