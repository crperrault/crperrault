A case in Court of Queen’s Bench of Montreal, 1879. The court rejected the judgement of the lower court that annulled a donation of $10,000 made by Clara Symes to some of her relatives (only identified as Cuvillier), as part of a larger donation of $100,000 to other relatives, after she married in 1872, and continued to pay interest on the donations till 1876. Symes and her husband, the duke of Bassano, argued that the grants were void once she married and had children. A change in the interim in the laws of Canada made such donations irrevocable.
