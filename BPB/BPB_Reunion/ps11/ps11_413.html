Notes for Dr. Luc-Hyacinthe Masson
[DBC] Doctor. Exiled to Bermuda in 1838 by Lord Durham after the Patriotes were defeated at St-Eustache. Returned to New York State in 1838 after the disavowal by the British parliament of Durham's proclamation. Opened a store at Fort Covington in partnership with Eustache Masson, his uncle who had been compromised and ruined by the rebellion. Went into trade in St Anicet with his brother Damien. Collector of customs at Dundee in 1844. MLA for Soulanges 1854-7. Conservative MP for Soulanges 1867-72. 6 children, only two of whom survived him. Named on the southwest corner of the Monument des Patriotes in Cote-des-Neiges cemetery

[Fauteux] Le jour de la bataille de St-Eustache, il tenta, sans succès, de ramener Girod qui tentait de fuir le combat. Deux de ses nombreux enfants lui survécurent: Louis Napoléon Masson de St-Anicet et Mme E Prieur de Coteau-Landing.
