Notes for Rt. Hon. Sir Allan Napier Macnab
16 Premier of Upper Canada 1854-6. Speaker of Legislative Assembly of
Upper Canada 1837-40. Commander of "the men of Gore" during the 1837
rebellion. Knighted 1838. Tory. Baronet 1858.

9 Macnab was associated with Bishop Strachan in the movement that resulted in the founding of Trinity College 1850-2. On his death it was announced that he had died in the communion of the Roman Catholic Church, that of his second wife. He was buried according to catholic rites, among considerable disorder, according to contemporary accounts of that funeral. The body was first interred in the family vault on the grounds of DundurnCastle, whence it was removed, with others, to the cemetery of the Holy Sepulchre, may 19 1909, when the city of Hamilton acquired the vault and the grounds immediately surrounding it.
