Notes for Richard Atkinson
1974 [will] Of Fareton. A stalwart of early days of Quakerism.
1678. Fined under “Popish recusants” Act. Distress a Mare worth £5
1678. Fined for attending Meeting at Ackonthwaite. Distress two cows worth £7.5.0
1682. Fora Meeting held in his barn, Distress two oxen, a cow and 5 other beasts value £25.3.4
1684. For absence from National Worship at 12d per Sunday. Goods value 16/-
1684. For presence at a Meeting at house of John Hudson in Hale. Fie 10/- ad further sum of £10 towards the £20 Forfeiture of the Stranger “who took upon himself to preach in the said conventicle”
1684. For a Meeting held in hiw own house for permitting the Meeting £20 and presence at it 10/-. Distressi in Cattle and Corn value £39.14.0 (to cover a share of those whose fines could not be distrained)
1681-1690 For Tithes. Corn and other things £25.7.3
