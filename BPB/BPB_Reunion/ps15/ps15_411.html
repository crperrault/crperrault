Notes for George Okill Stuart
1491Church of England clergyman. His education probably began in his father's schools, first in Montréal, then in Kingston. Studied at Union College (Schenectady NY) in 1789-90, then King's College (Windsor NS). Usher in Québec City grammar school 1794, Harvard College 1798, AB 1801 MA 1813. Ordained deacon 7-6-1800 by Bishop Mountain, and priest 22-8-1801. Appointed missionary in Toronto 1801. Chaplain to Legislative Council ca 1807. Master of Home District Grammar School in York 1807. Exponent of Joseph Lancaster's school system in which older students help instruct younger ones.

9 DCL Hon. Causa King’s College, Windsor, NS 1827.

In 1812 he succeeded his father as incumbent in Kingston and was succeeded at York by John Strachan, who had hoped to take over at Kingston instead. Archdeacon of York 1821. When the archdeaconry was divided in 1827, Stuart was named archdeacon of Kingston and Strachan archdeacon of York. He remained archdeacon of Kingston after the diocese of Toronto was created in 1839, with Strachan as its first bishop. DCL(Hon) from King's College, Windsor, 1827.

In his private life, Stuart was a quiet man, but he could be roused to determined action. To John Strachan, his bishop after 1839, he was a person who had to be "treated with honor and delicacy".

When the diocese of Ontario was created in 1862, Stuart was appointed its first dean by Bishop John Travers Lewis.

He had two sons and two daughters from his first marriage to Lucy Brooks.

He died at the age of 86.

He built three houses in Kingston, one of which still (1922) stands on King St. The second is now the house of the principal of Queens. He laid out the streets called Arch, Deacon, George, Okill, and possibly Stuart.
