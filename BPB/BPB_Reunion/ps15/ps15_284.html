Notes for John Shannon
John Shannon was born between 1833 and 1836 in St. Helens, Lancashire, the third of four children of Patrick Shannon, a labourer, and his wife Sarah. By 1841, his parents have moved to Wigan where, in 1851, he is an agricultural labourer living with his widowed mother on Lyon Street in Wigan.

Ellen Naylor was born around 1838 in Ashton in Makerfield, Lancashire, the second of five children of Abraham Naylor, a chemist and winnowing machine maker, and his wife Elizabeth.

John and Ellen were married on 8 October 1855 at St Oswald’s Chapel in Ashton in Makerfield.

John then became a stone mason, an occupation he practiced the rest of his life. After about 1857, they moved to Wigan, where they are living at 2, Chapel St in 1861. By 1868, they are in Pemberton, now in greater Wigan.

John and Ellen had six childen: William (1856), Theresa (1861), Sarah Ann (1863), Tracey (1866), Ellen (1868) and Liddia (1872).

Ellen died shortly before September 1872 in Wigan. Shortly before September 1876, John married Eliza Jane Dickinson, born around 1855, daughter of John Dickinson and his wife Elizabeth, of Bolton, Lancashire. They had a daughter, Elizabeth, in 1878 in Pemberton.

In 1881, John and Eliza Jane Shannon are at 5, Peet St. in Wigan.

They are not found in the 1891 census. John Shannon probably died in 1886 or 1888 in Wigan. Now living at 5 Peet St are Ellen and her husband, Thomas Little, their two children Ellen and John, and Ellen’s sister, Lydia Shannon.
