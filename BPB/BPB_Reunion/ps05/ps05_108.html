796 "At Russeltown, on the 12th instant, John Davidson, Esq., Collector of Customs, aged 76 [b 4/1779-4/1780]. This venerable and most respected gentleman emigrated from Dundee, Scotland, to this Province in 1817. He was one of the first settlers in the Township of Dundee, and gave it its name. During the Rebellion he raised a regiment of Volunteers to serve the Queen. He was a Magistrate and a Colonel of the Militia, and died old in years and full of honours. His decease is much regretted."
