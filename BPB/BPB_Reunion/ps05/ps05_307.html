Notes for Richard Denis De Fronsac
[DBC] Administrator, colonizer, trader, and fisherman. Richard took over the management of his father's affairs after the latter's departure for France, where he went looking for a publisher for his book and seeking support for his undertakings in Acadia. On his father's death in 1688, he petitioned to have his father's rights as governor -- which in fact he himself had exercised for 18 years -- conferred upon him. The government seems to have complied with this request since his jurisdiction extended over the whole area until his death in 1691. He promoted the settlement of Restigouche, including the establishment of Jesuit and Recollet missions.

He first married an Indian girl, Anne Parabego, by whom he had a daughter and a son. He then married Francoise Cailleteau of Québec. He died at 37 in the wreck of the St-Francois-Xavier in 1691. His estate was inherited by the children of his widow's subsequent mariage to Pierre Rey Gaillard.
