My query was posted on County of Huntingdon Quebec rootsweb. My
interest is in William Frederick Davidson b. 1851? probably Montreal,
eldest son of John Davidson and Margaret Hingston d. 8 August 1891
Toronto ON age 40. Buried Mount Royal Cemetery, Montreal QU Section
G850. His grandfather Colonel John Davidson immigrated to Lower
Canada about 1820. His father John Davidson (1812?-1899) served as a
captain in the militia regiment raised by Colonel Davidson for the
Huntingdon District and passed his enthusiasm for military life to his
son. In 1846 he married Margaret Hingston (1827-1909), the sister of
Sir William Hales Hingston (1829-1907), Montreal physician and mayor.
For some years he was store keeper at the Bruce Mines and later Silver
Islet for the Montreal Mining Co. During this lengthy period in the
Algoma region, some of his children took root there. His daughter
Ellen married Algoma pioneer T.A.P. Towers (q.v.) who lived in Thunder
Bay 1870-78. His son William was one of the earliest to arrive in PAL,
possibly 1870/71 when he worked for Thomas Marks in his general store.
In 1871 he was connected with the 3A Mining Company. In 1872 he took
charge of local business for the Collingwood line of steamers where he
remained 5 years before accepting a similar position with the North
West Transportation Co. (Beatty's Sarnia line) in June 1878. Davidson
was first elected to Shuniah council at a by-election 23 August 1879.
He served as reeve of Shuniah after PA's withdrawal in 1884. In 1882
he acted as local agent for American H.P. Weiland's lumber yard,
corner Cameron and Water Streets. By June 1884 he was PA harbour
master and appointed by the Macdonald government in 1885 to be one of
three local Dominion liquor commissioners, when the Federal government
unsuccessfully challenged Ontario's jurisdiction in this area. He was
partner in a firm which contracted to handle CPR freight arriving at
the Government Dock during the 1885 shipping season when about 40
freighthandlers struck, fearing the firm had bid too low to pay their
wages. Davidson visited his parents in Montreal at the beginning of
1887, was re-elected chief of PA fire brigade in March and, as color
sergeant of the Algoma Rifles, attended the spring militia school. He
was already reported to be suffering from "an attack of inflammatory
rheumatism". In April 1888, for unknown reasons, John Hancock and W.J.
Inglis (q.v.) took over the Beatty shipping agency. His brother James
A. (Jack) Davidson, a consumptive, died at PA 30 Dec 1888. Davidson
drops from view after this sad event. At the time of his death from
rheumatism in Toronto, his parents were living in Montreal at 9 Bishop
St. The Herald referred to him as "a jolly comrade and good
neighbour". Another brother, John Grant Davidson, said to be the
second son, died at his father's residence 9 Bishop Street 11 Sept
1895 age 32.
Affiliations: Presbyterian but attended Anglican church, Conservative
