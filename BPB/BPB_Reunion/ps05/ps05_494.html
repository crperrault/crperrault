Notes for Adrien Du Chesne
43 Chirurgien. Vint au Canada avant 1620, avant son neveu Charles LeMoyne. Chirurgien de l'habitation a Québec en 1634, puis a Trois Rivières en 1635-6. Concéda une terre à Abraham Martin.

[DBC] Although Sulte and Maude Abbott claim that he came to Québec in 1618, there is no evidence of his presence until 1631 when his name appears on the certificate of baptism of Elizabeth, daughter of Guillaume Couillard. In 1637, he acquired a grant of land on the outskirts of Québec, confirmed in 1639 by a deed from la Cie des Cent Associes. In 1641 he was at Dieppe, and back in Québec in 1645. In 1645, he turned over to Abraham Martin the land on what are now the Plains of Abraham. He is cited in Québec again in 1648. In a notary's deed from Dieppe in 1656, mention is made of "Maistre Adrien du Chesne, a surgeon at present living on the island of Guadeloupe". Is this where he ended his career?
