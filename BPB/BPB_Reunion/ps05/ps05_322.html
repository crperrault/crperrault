Notes for Joseph dit Jacques Denys
En septembre 1677, il fit profession et devint le premier Recollet canadien.

[DBC] Built a church and enlarged the missionaries' residence at Percé, in the seigneury partly owned by his father Pierre. Perhaps was the architect of the church on Ile Bonaventure across from Percé. He served as minister to the few permanent residents and to the summer population of fishermen. In 1689 he helped open a mission at Placentia, Newfoundland. In 1692 he founded the first Recollet convent in Montréal. It was in the church of this convent that took place "l'affaire des prie-dieu" during which Mgr St.Vallier, the bishop, and M. de Callière, the governor, fought over whose prie-dieu was in the best position, and which led to Mgr St.Vallier placing a personnal interdiction on the Recollets of Montréal, on pain of excommunication. The Recollets ignored it, the matter went before the Conseil Souverain of Québec, and then to the council of state in France, who declared that the bishop had acted beyond his authority.

In 1699, he ceased being guardian of the convent and became provincial commissioner of the mission for five years, when he became an ordinary religious, as parish priest at Cap-St-Ignace and St-Michel. In 1709 he became guardian and priest of the parish of Trois-Rivières, where he remained till 1717. In 1722, he went to Ile Royale for a year, returning there in 1727 as vicar-general of Mgr St- Vallier. He returned to Québec in 1729 where he died in 1736 at the age of 79.
