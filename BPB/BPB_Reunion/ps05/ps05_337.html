Notes for Paul Denys De St-Simon
[Jette] Conseiller au Conseil Superieur de Québec 1710.

[DBC] provost of the marshalcy, member of the Conseil Souverain.

In 1671-2 he accompanied Père Albanel from Tadoussac to James Bay for the purpose of claiming territory for France and christianizing the Indians. He planted the arms of France at Lake Nemiskau.

In 1678 he was appointed lieutenant of the Prévôté des Maréchaux. The Prévôté was a body of law enforcement officers, called "archers". He relinquished his appointement to his son Charles Denys de St- Simon in 1714. He was appointed to a temporary position to the Conseil Souverain in 1710, and a permanent one in 1717. Towards the end of that year he was acting as attorney-general. He also had farming and fur trading interests.
