Notes for Louis Hébert
[Jette] Apothicaire. Premier voyage en Acadie en 1604. Vient à Québec avec sa femme en 1617.

[DBC] For many years, he was the only man besides Champlain himself who took interest in cultivating land. The trading company did their utmost to discourage him. Appointed king's attorney 1620. He enjoyed the confidence of the Indians. Received title to his land in 1623. Known later as the fief Sault-au-Matelot, it included the sites presently occupied by the Basilica, seminary, Hebert and COuillard streets. Fief de Lespinay, along the St-Charles river, added later.

He fell on the ice in the winter of 1626 and died in 1627, to be buried in the Recollet cemetery. His bones were later transferred to the Recollet chapel.
