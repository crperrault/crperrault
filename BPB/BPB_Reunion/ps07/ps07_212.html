Notes for Jean Paul Godefroy
[DBC] Interpreter, trading clerk at Trois-Rivières, businesman, comptroller-general of the Communaute des habitants, naval commander and counsellor.

Probably settled in 1626, possibly in 1623. Returned to France with Champlain in 1629, may have returned in 1633, certainly byy 1636. The "Relations" allude to his apostolic zeal among the Indians, over whom he had great influence by virtue of his complete mastery of their language and his athletic prowess. One of the leading members of the Communaute des Habitants, he was involved in the fur trade, sealing and cod fisheries. He was entrusted in 1651 with the delicate mission of meeting with the repreentatives of the New England colonies. The negotiations, aimed at establishing commercial relations, failed because of the refusal of New England to agree to join New France against the Iroquois.

Godefroy returned to France after 1657 with his wife, Madeleine Legardeur de Repentigny and his daughter Barbe. His second daughter Charlotte stayed in Québec where she joined the Ursulines.
