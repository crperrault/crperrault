Notes for Thomas Godefroy De Normanville
[DBC] Interpreter, fluent in Algonquian, Iroquoian, and probably Huron. On three occasions he fell to the hands of the Iroquois. In 1641 and 1648, he escaped without too much difficulty. In 1652, the Iroquois carried him away to their cantons and killed him. Along with Father Buteux and three other white men, he was the first white man to travel up the St-Maurice River as far as the territory of the Attikamegues.
