This from the 27 September 1969 issue of the Armed Forces Journal, a copy of which I found when I was searching fruitlessly for the OXCART chronology:
"The DoD Deputies: Focus on the Men Who Run the Pentagon...Electronics...Herbert D. Benington, Room 3e110, Tel: OX5-7345.
"Mr. Benington serves as Acting Deputy Director of Defense Research and Engineering for Electronics and Information Systems in addition to his post as Assistant Director for Command and Control. In his Deputy post he manages DoD R&E programs in communications, electronics, command and control, intelligence, reconnaissance and electromagnetic warfare.
He was born in Montreal, Canada, in 1928. After receiving an SBEE from Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1950, he attended Oxford University as a Rhodes Scholar and received a BA (Oxon) in Mathematics in 1952. Prior to joining DoD in 1963, he worked on computer design and programming for the SAGE Air Defense System at MIT's Lincoln Laboratory, at the Rand Corp., and at the System Development Corp. Between 1960 and 1963 he directed research on various command and control problems and was a consultant to the Secretary of Defense and to the AF Scientific Advisory Board. From 1963 to 1965 he served as Technical Director for Joint Command and Control Requirements Group in the office of JCS, and from 1965 to 1967 he was Director for Command Control Communications and Intelligence in the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Systems Analysis."
