[Tony Manasseh, personal communication] She was known as Aunt Hettie. She, Dorothy and George left Lebanon at the onset of WW I for London as British were asked to leave when lebanon, under the Turks, was not with allied forces. They stayed there and made their lives there. Dr. ANtonios (husband and father) remained in Lebanon and practiced medicine (graduated from Leeds University in 1905) and took frequent trips to London to see his family. Christopher Naish later wrote a biography of Dr. Antonios and his remarkable work during WW1 and after. Died in 1929.
