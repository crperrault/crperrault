354 [Obit, NYT] George A Benington, chairman of the executive committee of the Perkin-Elmer Corporation of Norwalk, Conn, maker of analytic instruments and precision electronic components, died here yesterday in a physician’s office. He was 70 years old.

Mr. Benington lived at New Canaan, Conn., and also had a residence at 116 East 63d Street. He was a vice president of the Allied Chemical Corp until retirement from that post in 1960.

Mr Benington started his career with W. R. Grace & Co. Later he was president of the Bowker Chemical Co, a subsidiary of the American Agricultural Chemical Co., and vice-president of the parent company. In 1933 Mr Benington joined the Mutual Chemical Company of America, a subsidiary of the Allied Chemical and Dye Corp. and served as its president from 1943 to 1956.

He leaves his wife, Mrs Agnes Bigelow Benington; a son. Arthur; a daughter, Mrs William D Evers; two brothers, Edward and Herbert, and four grandchildren.
