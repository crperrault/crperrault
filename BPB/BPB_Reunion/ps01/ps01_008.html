In James Boswell's "Life of Samuel Johnson, LL.D." (1791) there are
a number of reference to the Boswells of Auchinleck which may be of use
to you (the page numbers are from the 1952 Great Books Edition):

p. 274 - "In the year 1504, the barony or manour of Auchinleck
(pronounced Affleck,) in Ayrshire, which belonged to a family of the
same name with the lands, having fallen to the Crown by forfeiture,
James the Fourth, King of Scotland, granted it to Thomas Boswell, a
branch of an ancient family in the county of Fife, stiling him in the
charter dilecto familiari nostri; and assigning as the cause of the
grant, pro bono et fideli servito nobis proestitio. Thomas Boswell was
slain in battle, fighting along with his Sovereign, at the fatal field
of Floddon, in 1513."

p. 274 - The estate descended from this Thomas in a direct male line to
David Boswell who left no son but four daughters, the eldest of whom
married Lord Cathcart [per CP III: 104 Alan Cathcart, 6th Lord Cathcart
m. in or bef. 7/1643 to Marion, dau. of David Boswell, of Auchinleck,
co. Ayr, by Isabel, dau. of Sir John Wallace, of Cairnhill.]
David's nephew, son of his next brother inherited the estate but due
to it being encumbered with debts was forced to sell a considerable part
of it. His son was an eminent lawyer who reacquired many of the lands
which had been sold. His son was the father of Boswell the biographer
and was one of the Judges of Scotland.

p. 493 - Boswell's father, Alexander Boswell, Lord Auchinleck [called so
because of him being a Judge of Scotland] died on 30 Aug 1782.

pp. 438-39 - Boswell had a younger brother named David who was soon to
return from Spain in April of 1780.

Boswell's wife, who is mentioned throughout, was Margaret Montgomerie.

There children were:
Alexander, David, James & Veronica
