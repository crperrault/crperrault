161An Honore Bailly patents 500 acres in vicinity of Orillia, Ontario in 1826

162 From the first it was manifest to the traders and settlers of a speculative turn of mind that the Indian trading post at The Narrows might naturally develop into a town at no very distant day. As already mentioned in our skecth of Hawkestone, or Hodegs' Landing, the landing places along the north shore of Lake Simcoe were various, but all were continuative of the great trail across the isthmus of Hurontario. In the immediate vicinity of The Narrows there were at least three of these landing places; the one on Lake Couchiching, where Orillia now stands; The Narrows proper, where the scattered hamlet of Invermara marks the place at which the important trail leading to Port Hope crossed the waters of Lake Simcoe; while the third was on the shore of Shingle Bay. Hence it was no wonder that the first white occupants of The Narrows and its environs were divided in their opinions as to the site of the coming emporium.

Honore Bailly patented, on September 1, 1826, five hundred acres in the vicinity of the town, viz., lots 10 and 12, in concession 5, (200 acres each) and lot 11, in concession 6, (west part, 100 acres). These lots were all beside the lakes, and were evidently selected on account of their proximity to the water.

By 1826 the Orillia area survey was completed and the Grants of Crown Land commenced. In 1826 the Crown granted 500 acres of land to Honore Bailley in south Orillia, in particular, 100 acres was the west half of Lot 11, Concession 6 which shores on what today is known as "Smith's Bay" In the late 1820's this particular 100 acres was purchased by my GG Grandfather Antoine Godard (Gaudaur), from Honore Bailley for the price of 25 pounds (approx $100). This purchase was officially registered on October 27, 1830 in the Township of Whitchurch, County of York.

Died -- At the residence of her son, George Baye, South Orillia, Ont the 20th of July, Julia, relict of the late Antoine Gaudaur, aged 82
Julia Sanscoucie was previously the wife of Honore Philippe /Bailly/Baye/Bailey who died in Mara Township Oct 12, 1829. Julia died July 20, 1880, 3rd Concession, South Orillia, Simcoe County.
Julia was married abt 1863 to Antione Gaudaur and probably lived with him for sometime at Fawn Bay, Rama Township before leaving, to live out her life with her son George in Orillia.
My records show Honore and Julia had at least 5 children.
Alexander, Francis, George (1814-1904), Pauline , Joseph (1829-1906). Honore and Antoine more than likely knew each other from the war of 1812 as both were posted at Drummond Island and then came to raise their families in Simcoe County.
I am a descendant of Antoine Gaudaur - Mary Shilling. Mary died Nov 27, 1848 in Orillia.

Drummond Island is just off Sault Ste Marie, Michigan, on Lake Huron.

Honore Bailly owned five hundred acres in the vicinity of what would become the town of Orillia, (200 acres each) lots 10 and 12, in Conc 5, and lot 11, 100 acres) in Conc 6. in September 1826.
The western half of lot 10 Conc 5 (south western half 95 acres) was purchased by Jacob Gill in 1833 from William Langhton, who had purchased it from the wife (Julia) of Honore Bailly, who was the oringinal grantee. Jacob Gill then proceed to built a fine wooden home, July 18, 1833, on the eastern side of the property on what would become "Gill Street", named after him, in Orillia. The other 5 acres of lot 10 Conc 5 was purchased by Jacob's son-in-law Thomas Atkinson.
Lot 11, Conc 6, (south western half) was purchased by Antoine Godard (Gaudaur) from Honore Bailly in 1827.
