Notes for Rev Edwin Sandys
2157 Archbishop of York 1577-1587. Born in the parish of Hawkshead, Furnace Fells, Lancs (is this the same Hawkshead?) doubtless at Esthwaite Hall, his father’s principal residence. Educated at St. John’s College, Cambs. Took orders and in 1547 became master of Catharine Hall, Held various ecclesiastical positions and was vice-chancellor of Cambridge University when Edward VI died in 1553. Was deeply tinctured with the principles of the reformation and supported the cause of Lady Jane Gray. For refusing to proclaim Mary at Cambridge he was committed to the Tower July 25, 1553 and a few weeks later removed to the Marshalsea, but was liberated and escaped to the continent in May 1554 where he remained until the accession of Elizabeth in 1558. He then returned to England and rose to great influence. Bishop of Worcester 21 Dec 1559, and in 1570 was promoted to Bishop of London. Consecrated Archbishop of York 8 March 1576-7. It is said that Elizabeth seriously considered appointing him arch of Canterbury in 1583 and the fact that he was a married alone prevented his elevation to the primacy. Died July 1588 and buried in Southwel Minster, Nottinghamshire. Figures prominently in ecclesiastical and political disputes of the era. See DNB (vol1 238), the intro by Ayre to “The sermons of Edwin Sandys DD” and “A history of the Sandys Family”
