1938 [History of Idaho, 1914, p 890]
Merchant, banker, organizer of several enterprises, official of the US Government service, and both in public and private life one of the strongest men of Idaho.
The father of his wife was an eminent lawyer and judge and the last official act of Pres Lincoln was the affixing of his signature to the appointment of Judge Kelly as a judge of the US district court.
Joseph Sr, who was a native of Canada and spent the early years of his life in Montreal, came to Idaho in 1867 and at Lewiston began a forwarding and commission business, selling it four years later and locating in Boise, where he spent the rest of his life. In 1886 he was one of the organizers of the Boise City National Bank and its asst cashier for 3 yrs, the appointed by Pres Harrison to the office of Receiver of the United States Land Office. In 1898 appointed by McKinley to office of US surveyor general, the functions of which he performed in this state until 1902.
He was active in politics, being in 1896 chairman of Republican party state central committee. In 1884 he bought the interest of the Boise Ditch Co and owned this irrigation canal till 1906. Owns large tracts of ranch lands, city real estate, and resides in one of Boise’s finest homes. Elk, of which he was twice exalted ruler. RC.
Married Kate A kelly, of Monroe WI. Five ch: Delphina (m Hugh Boyd of NYC), Joseph Jr, Louise (m Charles E Thum of Boise), and Miss Edna.

Jos Jr b Boise 1882 8 4. Went to Mt Tamalpais Military Academy (San Raphael CA), Oberlin College. Worked for fire insurance co in NYC, returned to Boise to his father’s office who was GM for southernID of Equitable Life Insurance. Left that office in 1904 to buy interest in Northrop Wholesale Hardware of Boise, trees till 1907, then sold. Bought into Peoples’ Co-Operative Mercantile Co as sec trees. Leading fire insurance and surety reps in Boise. Supervisor of 13th census from Oct 1909 to Jul 1910 for ID. Married Edythe E Ewing, day of James and Isabelle Ewing. Child Edythe Anabel.
