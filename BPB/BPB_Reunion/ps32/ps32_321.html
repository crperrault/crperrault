1999 [p 560] RECKITT, Ernest. certified public accountant; born Hull, England OCt 8 1866; son of Francis and Mary Ann (Benington) Reckitt; ed; Oliver’s Mount School, Scarborough Eng; matriculated at London Univ Jun 1883; student Owens Coll of Victoria Univ Manchester, 1883-7, BS second on list, with first class honors, 1887; M.S. degree received later; post-grad course Univ of Gottingen 1887-8; married Catherine Ruth Howard of London, Jan 18 1890; children Florence H, Dorothy C, Charles E, Margaret Ruth and Francis B. Came to America 1890 and located in Chicago; entered profession of public accountant 1891 in which he has since continued; senior partner firm of Ernest Reckitt & Co, principal office in Chicago, New York and Phila; also vice pres Union Drug and Mfg Co, Newark NJ; sec Independent Pure Ice CO, Buck and Rayner, Consumers Drug Co, all of Chicago and Turner Brass WOrks, Sycamore Ill. Mem. Ill. Soc of Certified Pub Accountants; one of the founders of the first soc of public accountants in Ill, under the Certified Public Accountants Act. Republican. Episcopalian. Mason. Author of several published addresses on accounting subjects, one of which, “The balance sheet from the banker’s standpoint” appeared recently in the Banker’s Magazine, NY. Recreations: golf, tennis. Thomas Walton opened a boarding school in Scarborough in 1864, and I was sent as a boarder , the youngest at the school, 9 1/2 years old. I was fearless or foolhardy when in the company of older boys, with the result that, on one occasion, I followed a bather, diving at the deep end of the bath, without any knowledge of swimming ; another time at school, seeing a boy swing himself off a high pole and alight on his feet, I tried the same and was carried into the house senseless. The school started with 11 boys, and grew to about 70. My son Malcolm was the youngest at the same school when Mr. Walton gave up. The premises in Belgrave Crescent were soon too small, and the school removed to Oliver's Mount, where, for some time, it was patronised by the boys from the principal families in the Society of Friends both in England and Ireland.
In 1867 the number of Thomas Walton's pupils increased so quickly that he bought a Hotel, known as the Princess Royal Hotel, altered the name to Oliver's Mount School, and when I left and went to York there were over 60 scholars.
The school afterwards passed into the hands of the Woodard Trust and became a successful girls' school, changing its name to Queen Margaret's.
At the age of 12 1/2 I passed on to Bootham School, York, for 3 1/2 years. Both schools encouraged study of Natural History and the exhibitions at the end of the year, of Fossils, Butterflies and Moths, Birds' Eggs and Pressed Flowers, were much ahead of most schools in the country.

TA Lee “US public accountancy firms and the recruitment of UK immigrants: 1850-1914”
Two UK immigrants formed a Chicago practice in 1892. Ernest Reckitt (UQ [CRP ie not a member of any UK professional society] and his brother, Charles Coleby Reckitt (UQ), started ERC [Ernest Reckitt and Co] following a year of employment with BWG in Chicago. Ernest was University of Manchester science graduate who came to the USA in 1890. Charles was a lawyer who worked in real estate in the USA from 1883. He remained with ERC until moving to industry in 1902. ERC’s initial employees from the US were, in 1906, Hugh Thornton Cuthbert (SAE, 1901) and Malcolm MacGregor (SAE, 1902).

[Barrow Wade and Guthrie (BWG) was founded in New York in 1883 as the US agency of Thomas, Wade and Guthrie (TWG) of London and Manchester. BWG merged in 1953 with the predecessor of the curent KPMG.

Arthur W Smith (UQ) was an articled clerk with TWG in Manchester in 1888, migrated to BWG in NY that year, and opened its Chicago office in 1890, thus starting the process of establishing national firms in the USA. Smith became a partner with Ernest Reckitt in Chicago in 1891.

George Wilkinson worked in Shanghai for a UK bank before joining VV in NY in 1887. He moved to BWG in 1889 and was its Chicago manager from 1891 to 1897. He then formed a partnership in Chicago with Ernest Reckitt who had practiced there on his own account since 1890.
